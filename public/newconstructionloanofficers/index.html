Frequently Asked New Construction Loan Questions

Clear answers about construction financing, builder loans, and new home build processes.

A construction-to-permanent loan (also called a one-time close construction loan) finances both the construction phase and the permanent mortgage in a single loan with one closing. You lock your interest rate before construction starts, make interest-only payments during the build, and the loan converts to a traditional mortgage when construction is complete. Connect with verified construction loan officers through Browse Lenders to compare construction-to-permanent options.

Construction loans fund the building process with money released in stages (draws) as construction progresses—requiring interest-only payments during construction and converting to principal + interest after completion. Traditional mortgages fund completed homes with fixed monthly principal and interest payments from day one. Construction loans have higher rates, stricter requirements, and more complexity than traditional purchase loans.

Most construction loans require 10-20% down, though some lenders offer construction-to-permanent financing with as little as 5% down for qualified buyers. Larger down payments reduce construction loan rates and improve approval odds. If you own a home with equity, you can use cash-out refinance proceeds as your down payment for new construction financing.

Construction loan officers use your middle credit score to determine rate pricing. Construction loans typically require 680+ credit for approval, with 720+ scores qualifying for the best rates. Lower scores face higher rates and stricter down payment requirements. Understanding your middle score before applying helps you set realistic expectations and improve your score if needed to unlock better construction financing terms.
