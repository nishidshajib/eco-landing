Best New Hampshire Credit Unions for Mortgages: Service CU vs Granite State vs St. Mary's

New Hampshire credit unions consistently offer mortgage rates 0.125%–0.25% below retail banks due to non-profit structure and member-focused pricing. The three largest NH mortgage credit unions are Service Credit Union, Granite State Credit Union, and St. Mary’s Bank.

Why NH Credit Unions Have Better Rates

Credit unions are non-profit member cooperatives:

  • No shareholder profit requirements = tighter margins on loans
  • Member surplus returned via better rates and lower fees
  • Lower overhead than retail banks (fewer branches, lean staffing)
  • Community-focused vs profit-maximizing

Typical NH credit union rate advantage: 0.125%–0.25% below Bank of America, TD Bank, or Citizens Bank on conventional and FHA loans.

Service Credit Union (Portsmouth HQ)

Service CU is NH’s largest credit union with $10B+ assets and strong mortgage presence.

Service CU Advantages:

  • Best conventional pricing in NH: Often 0.20%–0.25% below retail banks
  • Statewide branches: Portsmouth, Manchester, Concord, Dover, plus 30+ NH locations
  • Military affiliation: Originally DoD employees, now open to all NH residents
  • Relationship discounts: 0.125% rate discount with checking + direct deposit
  • In-house servicing: Loans serviced locally (you pay Service CU, not sold to Fannie)

Service CU Rate Examples (Jan 2026):

Conventional 30-year:

  • 740+ credit, 20% down: 6.125% (Bank of America: 6.375%)
  • 680–739 credit, 10% down: 6.375% (TD Bank: 6.625%)

FHA 30-year:

  • 680+ credit, 3.5% down: 5.875% (Citizens: 6.125%)

Service CU Membership:

  • Live, work, worship, or attend school in NH
  • $5 initial share deposit
  • Free checking account (no minimum balance required)

Best For:

  • Portsmouth/Seacoast buyers seeking lowest conventional rates
  • Military/DoD employees (extra perks)
  • Borrowers wanting local servicing vs national servicer

Granite State Credit Union (Statewide)

Granite State CU operates 15+ NH branches with competitive mortgage pricing and strong FHA programs.

Granite State CU Advantages:

  • Competitive FHA pricing: Often matches or beats Service CU on FHA
  • Flexible underwriting: Manual underwriting for credit challenges (620–680 scores)
  • Investor-friendly: Up to 4 financed properties (many CUs stop at 1–2)
  • NHHFA partnership: Administers NH Housing Finance Authority programs
  • Portfolio flexibility: Keeps some loans in-house for edge cases

Granite State CU Rate Examples (Jan 2026):

Conventional 30-year:

  • 740+ credit, 15% down: 6.250% (competitive but slightly higher than Service CU)

FHA 30-year:

  • 640–679 credit, 3.5% down: 6.000% (excellent for moderate credit)

VA 30-year:

  • 0% down: 5.875% (strong VA program)

Granite State CU Membership:

  • Live or work in NH
  • $25 initial deposit
  • Open to immediate family of existing members

Best For:

  • FHA borrowers with 640–680 credit scores
  • VA loans (competitive pricing)
  • Investors buying 2nd–4th properties
  • Borrowers needing manual underwriting flexibility

St. Mary’s Bank (Manchester HQ)

St. Mary’s Bank is America’s first credit union (founded 1909) with deep NH roots and strong portfolio lending.

St. Mary’s Advantages:

  • Portfolio lending division: Self-employed, unique properties, credit challenges
  • Jumbo portfolio loans: Up to $2M with in-house underwriting
  • Historical presence: 115+ years serving NH—deep local market knowledge
  • Manchester focus: Strong presence in southern NH (Manchester, Nashua, Salem)
  • Relationship banking: Personal service vs automated national lender experience

St. Mary’s Rate Examples (Jan 2026):

Conventional 30-year:

  • 740+ credit, 20% down: 6.250% (competitive with Granite State)

Portfolio jumbo:

  • $900k loan, 20% down, self-employed: 6.625% (vs 6.875%–7.00% at national lenders)

Portfolio bank statement loan:

  • Self-employed, 12-month statements: 6.750% (manual underwriting)

St. Mary’s Membership:

  • Live, work, or worship in NH
  • $5 membership fee + $25 savings account
  • Immediate family of members eligible

Best For:

  • Self-employed borrowers needing portfolio underwriting
  • Jumbo loans with manual underwriting flexibility
  • Manchester/southern NH borrowers preferring local service
  • Unique property types (seasonal homes, condo conversions)

How to Join NH Credit Unions

All three major NH credit unions have easy membership requirements:

  1. Open savings account ($5–$25 initial deposit)
  2. Verify NH connection (live, work, worship, or attend school in state)
  3. Apply for mortgage (same underwriting as banks, just better rates)

Many NH residents qualify for all three credit unions—get quotes from each to compare.

Credit Union Underwriting Standards

NH credit unions follow Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac guidelines for agency loans:

  • Minimum 620 credit (some offer manual underwriting down to 580)
  • DTI up to 50% with strong compensating factors
  • 3%–5% down on conventional (same as banks)
  • FHA 3.5% down, VA 0% down

Difference from banks: Credit unions typically have fewer overlays:

  • Banks add restrictions (minimum 680 credit, max 45% DTI)
  • Credit unions stick closer to agency minimums
  • Better approval odds for 620–680 credit scores

Portfolio Lending at NH Credit Unions

While most CU mortgages are sold to Fannie/Freddie, some maintain portfolio divisions:

St. Mary’s Bank:

  • Best NH credit union for portfolio lending
  • Self-employed bank statement loans
  • Jumbo up to $2M with manual underwriting
  • Investment properties 5–10 financed

Granite State CU:

  • Selective portfolio for unique scenarios
  • Good for 4th investment property (banks often cap at 4)
  • Manual underwriting for credit challenges

Service CU:

  • Limited portfolio lending (mostly agency conforming)
  • May keep loans in-house for servicing but still follow agency rules

Credit Union Service Differences

Advantages:

  • Local underwriting (knows NH market nuances)
  • In-person service at branches (less common with national lenders)
  • Loan officers are salaried (not commission) = less sales pressure
  • Local servicing (pay at branch or online, talk to NH-based reps)

Trade-offs:

  • Slower technology adoption (less digital than Rocket/Better)
  • Smaller teams may mean longer processing (30–40 days vs 20–25 for national lenders)
  • Limited evening/weekend hours vs 24/7 online lender support

Which NH Credit Union Should You Choose?

Choose Service CU if:

  • You want absolute lowest conventional rates (740+ credit)
  • Portsmouth/Seacoast location (convenient branches)
  • You value in-house servicing vs national servicer
  • Standard W-2 income, strong credit, conventional/FHA/VA

Choose Granite State CU if:

  • FHA or VA loan (competitive pricing)
  • Credit score 620–680 (good manual underwriting)
  • Buying 2nd–4th investment property
  • Need NHHFA program access

Choose St. Mary’s Bank if:

  • Self-employed with complex income
  • Jumbo loan needing manual underwriting
  • Investment property portfolio (5+ properties)
  • Manchester/southern NH location preference
  • Unique property type (seasonal, condo conversion)

Best Strategy: Get All Three Quotes

Smart NH borrowers get quotes from:

  1. Service Credit Union (benchmark for best conventional rate)
  2. Granite State CU (compare FHA/VA and investor pricing)
  3. St. Mary’s Bank (if self-employed or portfolio loan needed)

Compare APR (not just rate) and choose based on total cost + service fit. Credit union membership is free/cheap—no reason not to join all three and compare.

The Bottom Line on NH Credit Union Mortgages

New Hampshire credit unions deliver 0.125%–0.25% rate advantages over retail banks on conventional and FHA loans. Service CU typically offers best conventional pricing, Granite State excels at FHA/VA, and St. Mary’s provides strongest portfolio lending.

For most NH borrowers with standard W-2 income and good credit, credit unions beat banks and often match or beat national lenders—with added benefit of local service and in-state underwriting that understands New Hampshire market nuances.
