Hawaii Mortgage Rates by Island: Oahu vs Maui vs Kauai vs Big Island

Hawaii Mortgage Rates by Island: Oahu vs Maui vs Kauai vs Big Island

Hawaii mortgage rates vary more by island than most borrowers expect. Lender competition, property types, and second-home demand all influence pricing. Oahu tends to have the lowest rates due to volume and competition, while Maui and Kauai often price higher because of second-home and resort exposure. The Big Island sits between the two with more conforming loan activity and larger properties.

Why Island Differences Matter

Hawaii has distinct real estate markets on each island. Lenders price to risk, and risk varies by island:

  • Oahu has the largest population, most lender competition, and the deepest loan volume.
  • Maui includes resort markets like Wailea and Kapalua with high second-home demand.
  • Kauai has fewer lenders and limited inventory, increasing rate spreads.
  • Big Island has acreage properties and rural homes that require portfolio underwriting.

Oahu Mortgage Rate Characteristics

Oahu advantages:

  • Highest lender competition
  • More condo and single-family inventory
  • Faster appraisal turnaround

Oahu challenges:

  • Jumbo loans common in Honolulu coastal markets
  • Condo project approvals sometimes required

Maui Mortgage Rate Characteristics

Maui advantages:

  • Strong luxury market with specialized jumbo lenders
  • High borrower demand creates competitive jumbo offerings

Maui challenges:

  • Second-home and investment premium common
  • Limited lender count compared to Oahu
  • Resort condo documentation requirements

Kauai Mortgage Rate Characteristics

Kauai advantages:

  • Portfolio lenders comfortable with unique properties
  • Strong demand supports competitive offers for qualified borrowers

Kauai challenges:

  • Fewer lenders and higher fees
  • Appraisal complexity for oceanfront or rural properties
  • Vacation rental scrutiny

Big Island Mortgage Rate Characteristics

Big Island advantages:

  • More conforming loan volume
  • Larger land parcels and diverse property types

Big Island challenges:

  • Acreage and agricultural zoning require portfolio lenders
  • Appraisal variability for rural properties

How Rates Differ by Island

Common pricing patterns:

  • Oahu: Most competitive for conforming and FHA loans
  • Maui/Kauai: Higher rates and fees for second homes and vacation rentals
  • Big Island: Competitive conforming rates, portfolio needed for acreage

Tips to Secure the Best Hawaii Rate

  1. Compare APR, not just rate to capture fees and island pricing differences.
  2. Use a broker for jumbo and second-home pricing in Maui and Kauai.
  3. Lock rates early if buying in a resort market with limited lender options.
  4. Ask about occupancy pricing (primary vs second home vs investment).

Bottom Line

Hawaii mortgage rates are highly island-specific. Oahu borrowers benefit from the largest lender pool, while Maui and Kauai often require specialized lenders with second-home and jumbo experience. Compare multiple lenders and focus on APR to ensure you get the best total cost for your Hawaii home.
