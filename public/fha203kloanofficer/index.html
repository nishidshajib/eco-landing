Frequently Asked FHA 203(k) Questions

Clear answers about FHA 203(k) rehab loans, contractor requirements, and fixer-upper financing.

Standard FHA 203(k) loans finance unlimited renovation costs including structural repairs, foundation work, additions, and major systems—requires HUD consultant oversight, 3.5% down, and 640+ credit. Limited FHA 203(k) loans cover up to $35,000 in cosmetic repairs (painting, flooring, appliances, minor fixes) with no HUD consultant required, 3.5% down, and 620+ credit. Connect with verified FHA 203(k) loan officers through Browse Lenders to compare both programs.

Standard FHA 203(k) loans typically require 640+ credit score for 3.5% down (some lenders accept 580-639 with 10% down). Limited FHA 203(k) loans generally need 620+ credit. Your middle credit score determines your rate tier—higher scores (680-720+) qualify for better pricing. Understanding your middle score before applying helps you set realistic expectations and improve eligibility if needed.

Standard FHA 203(k) loans require a HUD consultant to review contractor bids, create renovation work plans, manage draw inspections, and ensure code compliance. Consultant fees typically range $800-$1,500. Limited FHA 203(k) loans (under $35,000 in repairs) do NOT require a HUD consultant—streamlining the process and reducing costs. Your FHA 203(k) loan officer determines which program fits your renovation scope.
