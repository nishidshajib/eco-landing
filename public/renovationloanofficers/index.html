Renovation Loan Programs for Every Home Improvement Project

Whether you're buying a fixer-upper or renovating your current home, the right renovation loan program makes all the difference.

FHA 203(k) Renovation Loans

Finance fixer-uppers and major renovations with FHA 203(k) loans—covering purchase price plus renovation costs (up to $35,000 for Limited 203k or unlimited for Standard 203k) in one loan. Perfect for buying distressed properties, major structural repairs, or full rehabs with lower down payment requirements (3.5% down) and flexible credit guidelines.

HomeStyle® Renovation Financing

Fannie Mae HomeStyle® renovation loans finance cosmetic and structural updates on primary residences, second homes, or investment properties—covering renovations, additions, energy improvements, and luxury upgrades. Understand how your middle credit score affects HomeStyle® approval and explore financing up to 75-97.5% of after-repair value depending on occupancy.

Cash-Out Refinance for Renovations

Already own your home? Use cash-out refinance to tap your equity and fund kitchen remodels, bathroom upgrades, additions, or major repairs—refinancing your existing mortgage and pulling out cash for renovations at potentially lower rates than personal loans or HELOCs.

Frequently Asked Renovation Loan Questions

Clear answers about renovation financing, contractor requirements, and home improvement readiness.

FHA 203(k) loans are government-insured and allow 3.5% down with flexible credit (typically 620+ for Limited 203k, 640+ for Standard 203k). They are ideal for buying fixer-uppers or major structural repairs but have stricter contractor requirements and HUD consultant oversight. HomeStyle® renovation loans (Fannie Mae) allow higher loan amounts, financing for luxury upgrades and investment properties, and simpler contractor coordination—but typically require 5-20% down and 620-680+ credit depending on loan-to-value. Renovation loan officers through Browse Lenders help you compare both options based on your renovation scope and financial profile.

Yes—if you already own your home, you can use a cash-out refinance to pull equity for renovations, or in some cases a "refinance with renovation" HomeStyle® loan. Cash-out refinance replaces your existing mortgage with a new larger loan, giving you cash for home improvements. This works well if current rates are competitive or you want to consolidate high-interest debt alongside renovation funding. Your renovation loan officer can compare cash-out refinance vs. HELOC vs. home improvement loans to determine the best financing structure for your remodel.

FHA 203(k) loans have strict contractor requirements—contractors must be licensed, insured, and approved by your lender or HUD consultant. Standard 203(k) loans require a HUD consultant to oversee the project, review contractor bids, and manage draw inspections. HomeStyle® renovation loans have more flexible contractor requirements but still need licensed, insured contractors with detailed renovation bids. Cash-out refinance has no specific contractor requirements since you receive cash directly—but renovation loan officers recommend using licensed contractors to protect your investment and ensure quality work.

Renovation loan officers use your middle credit score to determine rate pricing. FHA 203(k) loans typically require 620-640+ credit (depending on loan type and down payment), while HomeStyle® renovation loans generally need 620-680+ scores. Cash-out refinance requirements vary by lender and loan-to-value but typically need 640-680+ credit. Higher scores (720-740+) qualify for better rates across all renovation loan types. Understanding your middle score before applying helps you set realistic expectations and improve your score if needed to unlock better renovation financing terms.
