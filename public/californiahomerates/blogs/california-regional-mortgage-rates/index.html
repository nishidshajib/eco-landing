Regional California Mortgage Rates: Bay Area vs LA vs San Diego vs Inland Empire (2026)

Why California Mortgage Rates Vary by Region

California is not a single mortgage market—it’s six distinct regional markets with different lender competition, property values, and rate pricing:

  1. Bay Area (SF, San Jose, Oakland) — Lowest rates (6.375%–6.625% conforming, 6.875%–7.125% jumbo)
  2. Los Angeles & Orange County — Very competitive (6.375%–6.75% conforming, 6.875%–7.25% jumbo)
  3. San Diego — Competitive (6.50%–6.875% conforming, 7.00%–7.375% jumbo)
  4. Sacramento & Central Valley — Moderate pricing (6.50%–6.875% conforming, 7.125%–7.50% jumbo)
  5. Inland Empire (Riverside, San Bernardino) — Higher rates (6.625%–7.00% conforming, 7.25%–7.625% jumbo)
  6. Wine Country & North Coast (Napa, Sonoma, Mendocino) — Highest rates (6.625%–7.00% conforming, 7.25%–7.75% jumbo)

Rate spread across California: 0.25%–0.375% (Bay Area to Inland Empire for same borrower, same loan). On $900K loan, 0.25% = $156/month, $9,360 over 5 years. On $1.5M jumbo, 0.375% = $391/month, $23,460 over 5 years.

Why regional rate differences exist:

  • Lender competition: Bay Area has 60+ active lenders (brokers, banks, credit unions) competing for business → razor-thin margins. Inland Empire has 20 lenders → less competition, higher rates.
  • Loan volume: Bay Area lenders close 10x more loans than Inland Empire lenders → wholesale pricing discounts, economies of scale.
  • Borrower profiles: Bay Area tech workers (W2, 780+ credit, large down payments) = lower risk → lower rates. Inland Empire buyers (lower credit, smaller down payments) = higher risk → higher rates.
  • Property types: Bay Area = high-balance conforming + jumbo dominate (competitive pricing). Inland Empire = FHA/VA dominate (fewer lenders, less competition).

Bottom line: Where you buy in California affects your rate as much as your credit score. Shopping brokers (who access 40+ lenders statewide) eliminates regional rate disadvantages.

1. Bay Area (SF, San Jose, Oakland, Peninsula)

Market Overview

Median home price: $1.5M (San Francisco), $1.8M (San Jose/Palo Alto), $1.2M (Oakland)

Dominant loan types:

  • High-balance conforming ($800K–$1,149,825): 35% of market
  • Jumbo ($1.15M–$3M+): 45% of market
  • Conventional conforming (<$806,500): 15% of market
  • FHA/VA: <5% (median price too high for government loans)

Lender competition: Highest in California

  • 60+ mortgage brokers (specialize in tech worker stock comp, jumbo loans)
  • 40+ banks/credit unions (Wells Fargo, Chase, BofA, Union Bank, First Republic—RIP, local CUs)
  • 20+ wholesale lenders competing for broker business (UWM, Pennymac, Rocket Pro TPO)

Why Bay Area has lowest rates:

  1. Intense broker competition: 60+ brokers fighting for same borrowers → undercutting each other by 0.125%–0.25%
  2. High loan volume: Bay Area lenders close 15,000+ loans/month → wholesale lenders offer volume discounts
  3. Low-risk borrowers: Tech workers (W2, 780+ credit, 20%+ down, $200K+ income) = best pricing tier
  4. Jumbo lender concentration: 15+ jumbo lenders compete for $1.5M–3M loans → jumbo rates only 0.375%–0.50% above conforming (vs. 0.75% in Inland Empire)

Current Bay Area Mortgage Rates (January 2026)

Conforming (up to $806,500):

  • Broker wholesale pricing: 6.375%–6.50% (740+ credit, 20% down)
  • Bank retail pricing: 6.625%–6.75% (same borrower)
  • Spread: 0.25% (brokers undercut banks)

High-balance conforming ($806,500–$1,149,825):

  • Broker wholesale: 6.50%–6.625%
  • Bank retail: 6.75%–6.875%
  • Spread: 0.25%

Jumbo ($1.15M–$3M):

  • Broker wholesale: 6.875%–7.125% (740+ credit, 20% down)
  • Bank retail (Wells, Chase): 7.25%–7.50%
  • Portfolio lenders (relationship banking): 6.75%–7.00% (for high-net-worth clients with $5M+ investments)
  • Spread: 0.375% (brokers beat banks, portfolio beats brokers for ultra-wealthy)

Bay Area Rate Shopping Strategy

✅ Work with brokers: Bay Area brokers access 40+ wholesale lenders, compare rates daily, beat bank retail by 0.25%–0.375%

✅ Leverage tech worker programs: Brokers specializing in stock comp underwriting (RSUs, options, ESPP) get better jumbo rates (know how to count unvested RSUs, lenders trust their files)

✅ Check portfolio lenders for jumbo: If you have $5M+ in investments, Wells Fargo Wealth Management or BofA Preferred Rewards offer 6.75%–7.00% jumbo rates (0.25%–0.50% below retail)

✅ Rate-shop aggressively: Bay Area rate spread (highest to lowest lender) = 0.375%–0.50% on jumbo loans. Get Loan Estimates from 3–5 brokers + 1–2 banks, pick lowest APR.

Example: San Jose Tech Worker

  • Purchase price: $1.8M (San Jose)
  • Down payment: 25% ($450K, stock comp liquidation)
  • Loan amount: $1,350,000 (jumbo)
  • Credit: 780

Bank (Wells Fargo retail):

  • Rate: 7.375%, monthly payment: $9,370

Mortgage broker (shops 40 lenders, finds Pennymac jumbo):

  • Rate: 6.875%, monthly payment: $8,875

Savings: $495/month, $29,700 over 5 years

Bay Area takeaway: Brokers dominate Bay Area jumbo pricing. Banks can’t compete (retail margins too high). If you use bank, you overpay 0.375%–0.50%.

2. Los Angeles & Orange County

Market Overview

Median home price: $900K (Los Angeles County), $1.1M (Orange County)

Dominant loan types:

  • High-balance conforming ($800K–$1,149,825): 40% of market
  • Conventional conforming (<$806,500): 30% of market
  • Jumbo ($1.15M–$2M+): 20% of market
  • FHA/VA: 10% (first-time buyers, military)

Lender competition: Very high

  • 50+ mortgage brokers (generalists + jumbo specialists + Latino-focused lenders)
  • 35+ banks/credit unions (national banks + local CUs like Kinecta, Logix, Wescom)
  • 25+ wholesale lenders competing for broker business

Why LA/OC rates are competitive:

  1. Diverse lender base: National banks + local CUs + 50 brokers = intense competition
  2. High loan volume: LA/OC close 20,000+ loans/month (more than Bay Area) → economies of scale
  3. Broker market share: 60% of LA/OC borrowers use brokers (vs. 40% banks) → brokers drive pricing down

Current LA/OC Mortgage Rates (January 2026)

Conforming (up to $806,500):

  • Broker wholesale: 6.375%–6.625%
  • Bank retail: 6.625%–6.75%
  • Credit union: 6.50%–6.75% (members only, competitive with brokers)
  • Spread: 0.125%–0.25%

High-balance conforming ($806,500–$1,149,825):

  • Broker wholesale: 6.50%–6.75%
  • Bank retail: 6.75%–6.875%
  • Spread: 0.125%–0.25%

Jumbo ($1.15M–$2M):

  • Broker wholesale: 6.875%–7.25%
  • Bank retail: 7.125%–7.50%
  • Portfolio lenders: 6.875%–7.125% (relationship banking)
  • Spread: 0.25%–0.375%

LA/OC Rate Shopping Strategy

✅ Brokers + credit unions: Get quotes from 2 brokers (wholesale pricing) + 1 credit union (if you’re a member). Credit unions like Kinecta, Logix, Wescom offer competitive conforming rates (6.50%–6.75%, match brokers).

✅ Shop Latino-focused lenders (if Hispanic borrower): LA/OC has Spanish-speaking brokers specializing in Latino buyers (CalHFA, ITIN loans, foreign income documentation). Example: Mortgage Capital Associates, Latino Lending Network.

✅ Avoid retail banks for jumbo: Wells/Chase/BofA retail rates are 7.25%–7.50% (jumbo). Brokers get 6.875%–7.125% (0.375% lower).

Example: Orange County Purchase

  • Purchase price: $1.2M (Irvine)
  • Down payment: 20% ($240K)

  • Credit: 740

Bank (Chase retail):

  • Rate: 6.875%, monthly payment: $6,322

Credit union (Logix FCU, member):

  • Rate: 6.625%, monthly payment: $6,164

Mortgage broker (shops 40 lenders, finds UWM high-balance):

  • Rate: 6.50%, monthly payment: $6,084

Savings (broker vs. bank): $238/month, $14,280 over 5 years

LA/OC takeaway: Credit unions competitive for conforming (members only). Brokers win for high-balance + jumbo (0.25%–0.375% lower than banks).

3. San Diego

Market Overview

Median home price: $850K (San Diego County)

Dominant loan types:

  • High-balance conforming ($700K–$1,149,825): 35% of market
  • Conventional conforming (<$806,500): 25% of market
  • VA (0% down, military): 20% (highest VA market share in California, due to Navy/Marines presence)
  • FHA: 15%
  • Jumbo ($1.15M+): 5%

Lender competition: High (but less than Bay Area/LA)

  • 35+ mortgage brokers
  • 25+ banks/credit unions (Navy Federal, San Diego County CU, local banks)
  • 15+ wholesale lenders

Why San Diego rates are 0.125%–0.25% higher than Bay Area/LA:

  1. Less lender competition: 35 brokers (vs. 60 in Bay Area) → less rate undercutting
  2. Military market concentration: VA loans dominate (20% market share) → lenders focus on VA, less competition for conventional/jumbo
  3. Smaller jumbo market: Only 5% of loans are jumbo (vs. 45% in Bay Area) → fewer jumbo lenders, higher jumbo rates

Current San Diego Mortgage Rates (January 2026)

Conforming (up to $806,500):

  • Broker wholesale: 6.50%–6.75%
  • Bank retail: 6.75%–6.875%
  • Credit union (Navy Federal, SDFCU): 6.625%–6.875%
  • Spread: 0.125%–0.25%

High-balance conforming ($806,500–$1,149,825):

  • Broker wholesale: 6.625%–6.875%
  • Bank retail: 6.875%–7.00%
  • Spread: 0.125%–0.25%

VA (0% down, military):

  • VA specialists (Veterans United, Navy Federal, USAA): 6.125%–6.375%
  • General lenders: 6.375%–6.625%
  • Spread: 0.25% (VA specialists beat general lenders by focusing on military market)

Jumbo ($1.15M–$2M):

  • Broker wholesale: 7.00%–7.375%
  • Bank retail: 7.375%–7.625%
  • Spread: 0.375%

San Diego Rate Shopping Strategy

✅ VA loans: Use VA specialists: San Diego has highest VA loan volume in California (military bases). Veterans United, Navy Federal, USAA offer 6.125%–6.375% VA rates (0.25% below general lenders).

✅ Conforming: Shop brokers + Navy Federal: If you’re military/veteran, Navy Federal CU offers competitive conforming rates (6.625%–6.875%). If not military, brokers beat banks by 0.125%–0.25%.

✅ Jumbo: Brokers essential: San Diego jumbo market is small (5% of loans). Only 8–10 jumbo lenders active. Brokers access all of them, banks offer 1. Brokers win by 0.375%–0.50% on jumbo.

Example: San Diego Military Buyer (VA Loan)

  • Purchase price: $800K (Chula Vista)
  • Down payment: 0% (VA loan)
  • Loan amount: $800,000
  • Credit: 720

General lender (Wells Fargo):

  • Rate: 6.50%, monthly payment: $5,059

VA specialist (Veterans United):

  • Rate: 6.125%, monthly payment: $4,853

Savings: $206/month, $12,360 over 5 years

San Diego takeaway: VA specialists dominate military market (0.25% lower rates). Brokers win for conforming/high-balance (0.125%–0.25% lower than banks). Jumbo rates 0.125%–0.25% higher than Bay Area/LA (less competition).

4. Sacramento & Central Valley

Market Overview

Median home price: $525K (Sacramento), $450K (Fresno, Bakersfield, Modesto)

  • Jumbo ($1.15M+): <5%

Lender competition: Moderate

  1. Fewer lenders: 25 brokers (vs. 60 in Bay Area) → less competition
  2. Lower loan volume: Sacramento closes 3,000 loans/month (vs. 15,000 in Bay Area) → no volume discounts

6. Wine Country & North Coast (Napa, Sonoma, Mendocino)

Market Overview

Median home price: $750K (Napa), $850K (Sonoma), $550K (Mendocino)

Dominant loan types:

  • Second home/vacation property (40% of market, rates 0.375%–0.75% higher than primary residence)
  • Conventional conforming (<$806,500): 30%
  • Jumbo ($1.15M–$3M, wine estates): 20%
  • FHA/VA: <10%

Lender competition: Low-Moderate (fewest lenders in California)

  • 15+ mortgage brokers
  • 12+ banks/credit unions (local community banks, Redwood CU)
  • 10+ wholesale lenders

Why Wine Country/North Coast rates are highest in California:

  1. Fewest lenders: 15 brokers (vs. 60 in Bay Area) → minimal competition
  2. Second-home market: 40% of loans are vacation properties → lenders charge 0.375%–0.75% premium (higher default risk, non-owner-occupied)
  3. Small loan volume: Wine Country closes 500 loans/month (vs. 15,000 Bay Area) → no economies of scale

  • Spread: 0.25%

  • Spread: 0.25%

Jumbo ($1.15M–$3M, second home/vacation):

  • Bank retail: 7.875%–8.50%

✅ Avoid local banks for second homes: Local Wine Country banks charge 7.50%–8.00% second-home rates (0.50%–0.75% above brokers). Brokers get 7.00%–7.375% (0.50% lower).

✅ Jumbo: Shop Bay Area brokers: If buying $1.5M+ wine estate, work with Bay Area jumbo broker (they access 15+ jumbo lenders vs. 5 in Wine Country). Saves 0.25%–0.50% vs. local Wine Country lender.
