Why Compare California Mortgage Rates

Regional pricing, loan type tiers, and lender competition create 0.25%–0.75% rate spreads.

California is not one mortgage market—Bay Area, LA, San Diego, Sacramento, and Inland Empire have different lender competition and pricing. Brokers access 20–50 wholesale lenders statewide, banks offer one rate sheet.

Education-first approach: understand high-balance conforming limits, rate lock strategies, and APR vs. rate to avoid overpaying on large California loans.

High-Balance Conforming Savings

California's $1,149,825 conforming limit (LA, SF, OC, SD counties) saves 0.50%–0.75% vs. jumbo on $800K–1.15M loans.

  • $900K loan: high-balance conforming 6.625%, jumbo 7.25% (0.625% savings = $391/month)
  • $1.1M loan: high-balance conforming 6.75%, jumbo 7.50% (0.75% savings = $579/month)
  • Know California limits—lenders sometimes misprice high-balance as jumbo (overpay 0.50%–0.75%)

Regional Rate Competition

Bay Area brokers offer 0.25%–0.375% lower rates than Inland Empire banks. Lender concentration drives pricing.

  • Bay Area: 60+ lenders, conforming 6.375%–6.50%
  • Inland Empire: 20 lenders, conforming 6.625%–7.00%
  • Brokers access statewide wholesale lenders (same pricing whether you're in SF or Riverside)

Rate Lock Timing

California closings average 32 days. 30-day locks expire early, 60-day locks cost 0.25%. 45-day locks (+0.125%) are California standard.

  • 45-day lock costs $69/month on $800K loan, saves $347/month if rates rise 0.50%
  • Float-down options cost 0.125% for one-time re-lock if rates drop 0.375%+
  • Lock at application for 45 days (don't gamble on California rate volatility)
