Understanding VA Loan Benefits & Requirements

How VA loans work, who qualifies, and why VA loan officers deliver better outcomes than generic lenders

VA Entitlement & Certificate of Eligibility

Your VA loan entitlement determines how much you can borrow with zero down. Most veterans have full entitlement for loans up to conforming limits ($766,550 in 2025). Using a VA loan once does not eliminate entitlement—you can use VA loans multiple times throughout life. VA loan officers help calculate remaining entitlement after prior use and structure purchases accordingly.

VA Residual Income vs. DTI

VA loans use residual income (money left after all debts and living expenses) rather than just debt-to-income ratios. A 50% DTI might qualify if residual income is sufficient for family size and region. Understanding residual income tables matters more than generic DTI calculations—VA loan officers who specialize in this approve borrowers conventional lenders deny.

VA Funding Fee & Exemptions

VA loans charge a funding fee (1.25%-3.3% of loan amount) unless you are exempt due to disability compensation. Veterans with 10%+ disability ratings from VA skip the fee entirely—saving $2,500-$10,000 depending on loan size. VA loan officers ensure exemptions are claimed properly and help veterans understand how funding fees affect total costs.

VA Credit Score Requirements

VA does not set minimum credit scores—lenders do. Some VA specialists approve 580 scores while big banks require 640+. Your middle credit score (median of three bureau FICO scores) determines VA rate pricing.
