Texas Mortgage Broker Questions

Straightforward answers about how mortgage brokers work and how to shop transparently.

Brokers charge either origination fees (1%) paid by you or lender fees paid by the lender. Most borrowers prefer lender-paid compensation—the broker shops lenders and you pay nothing extra. Compare total costs (broker fees + lender origination fees) across offers to evaluate true pricing.

Typical timeline is 30-45 days from application to closing. Brokers can accelerate with portfolio lenders (15-20 days) or extend timelines if you need time to improve credit, save down payment, or coordinate with a home purchase.

Yes, brokers access lenders most homebuyers never find—portfolio banks, credit unions, jumbo specialists—exposing you to wider pricing. Brokers also leverage relationships for priority processing and rate discounts. Comparing broker offers to direct lender offers typically favors the broker.

Verify licensing through the Texas Workforce Commission and NMLS. Ask for references, compare competing offers with written terms, and avoid brokers who guarantee rates or pressure quick decisions. Transparent brokers provide clear fee breakdowns and explain why their offer is competitive.
