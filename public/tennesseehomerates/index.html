Tennessee Mortgage Programs & Rates

Compare loan programs available across Tennessee markets.

Conventional Mortgages (620+ Credit)

Competitive rates for Tennessee borrowers with good credit. 3-5% down payment options; no mortgage insurance at 20% down. Best rates currently 6.25-6.75% for qualified borrowers in Nashville and Memphis markets.

FHA Loans (3.5% Down, 580+ Credit)

FHA financing allows 3.5% down with flexible credit requirements. Tennessee FHA rates currently 6.50-7.00%. Popular with first-time buyers across Knoxville, Chattanooga, and smaller Tennessee markets.

VA Loans (0% Down for Veterans)

Zero down payment mortgages for Tennessee veterans and active military. Competitive VA rates 6.00-6.50% with no mortgage insurance required. Strong presence near Fort Campbell, Memphis Naval Support Activity.

Tennessee borrowers save by comparing conventional, FHA, VA, and jumbo programs. Rate differences of 0.25-0.50% translate to $50-100/month savings on typical Tennessee home.

Tennessee vs National Rate Comparison

Tennessee mortgage rates typically track national averages within 0.10-0.25%. Memphis and Nashville competitive with major metros; rural Tennessee may see 0.10% premium due to lower loan volumes.

Rate Locks & Timing in Tennessee

Lock rates for 30-60 days at no cost; extended locks (90+ days) cost 0.25-0.50%. Tennessee closing timelines typically 25-35 days; coordinate lock with expected closing date.

Credit Score Impact on TN Rates

Why Tennessee Mortgage Rates Are Competitive

Tennessee markets offer strong lending competition and affordable housing.

Tennessee benefits from competitive mortgage markets in Nashville, Memphis, Knoxville, and Chattanooga. Multiple national lenders, regional banks, and credit unions compete for Tennessee borrowers—resulting in competitive rates.

No state income tax and lower cost of living make Tennessee attractive for homeownership. Lower home prices vs coastal markets mean smaller loan amounts and lower monthly payments.

Competitive Rate Environment

Tennessee mortgage rates competitive with national averages. Nashville and Memphis metro areas attract national lenders; Knoxville and Chattanooga markets offer strong regional competition.

  • Conventional: 6.25-6.75% typical (740+ credit)
  • FHA: 6.50-7.00% typical (620+ credit)
  • VA: 6.00-6.50% typical (veterans)

Affordable Home Prices

Tennessee median home prices significantly below coastal markets. Nashville average $425K; Memphis $250K; Knoxville $325K; Chattanooga $310K. Lower prices mean smaller loans and lower monthly payments.

  • Nashville: $425K median (lower than Austin, Denver)
  • Memphis: $250K median (most affordable major TN market)
  • Knoxville/Chattanooga: $300-325K median

No State Income Tax Benefit

Tennessee has no state income tax on wages—increasing take-home pay and debt-to-income qualification power. More income available for housing payment vs high-tax states.

  • No state income tax on wages (Hall Tax only on investment income)
  • DTI qualification easier vs high-tax states
  • More take-home income for housing payment

Tennessee Mortgage Rates by Credit Score

Credit score significantly impacts Tennessee mortgage rates. Understanding rate tiers helps you estimate monthly payment and total borrowing cost.

Excellent Credit (740+)

Best Tennessee rates available. Conventional: 6.25-6.50%; FHA: 6.50-6.75%; VA: 6.00-6.25%. Fastest approval; lowest down payment requirements.

Good Credit (680-739)

Competitive Tennessee rates. Conventional: 6.50-6.75%; FHA: 6.75-7.00%; VA: 6.25-6.50%. Strong approval odds; typical rate tier for most borrowers.

Fair Credit (620-679)

Moderate Tennessee rates. Conventional: 6.75-7.25%; FHA: 7.00-7.50%. Higher down payment may improve rate. Consider credit improvement before applying.
