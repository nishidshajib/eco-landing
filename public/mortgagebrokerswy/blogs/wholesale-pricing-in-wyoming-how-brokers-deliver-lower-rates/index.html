Wholesale Pricing in Wyoming: How Brokers Deliver Lower Rates

Wyoming mortgage brokers provide access to wholesale pricing, which is typically 0.125%–0.375% lower than retail bank rates for the same lender. Understanding wholesale pricing helps borrowers save money and compare true costs.

What Is Wholesale Pricing?

Wholesale pricing is discounted mortgage rates offered to brokers who originate loans in volume.

Why it’s lower:

  • Lenders save on marketing costs (brokers bring clients)
  • Brokers submit complete, pre-qualified files
  • Lenders reward volume with lower pricing

Same Lender, Lower Rate

Example using a national lender:

  • Retail direct: 6.750% rate
  • Wholesale via broker: 6.500% rate

Savings: 0.25% = ~$76/month on a $450K loan

Wholesale Rate Sheet Access

Wyoming brokers use pricing engines like:

  • Optimal Blue
  • Mortech
  • LenderPrice

These systems compare 20+ lenders in real time, allowing brokers to identify the lowest APR combination for each borrower.

Wholesale Savings Example

$450K Conventional Loan

Lender Channel Rate APR Fees
Retail Bank 6.750% 6.95% $6,500
Broker Wholesale 6.500% 6.65% $2,250

Total Savings: ~$9,000 over 5 years (rate + fee difference)

When Wholesale Pricing Wins

  • Standard conforming loans
  • Strong credit (720+)
  • Borrowers comparing multiple lenders

When Wholesale May Not Win

  • Rural acreage requiring portfolio lenders
  • Credit union member pricing occasionally matches wholesale

Borrower-Paid vs Lender-Paid

Brokers can be compensated two ways:

  1. Borrower-Paid: Lower rate, upfront fee
  2. Lender-Paid: Higher rate, no upfront fee

Ask brokers to show both options and compare breakeven timelines.

Bottom Line

Wholesale pricing is the broker advantage. Wyoming borrowers often save 0.125%–0.375% on rates plus lower fees compared to retail banks.
