Why New Hampshire Borrowers Use Mortgage Brokers

New Hampshire mortgage brokers access wholesale lender networks that retail banks cannot offer. Instead of one bank’s pricing and underwriting rules, brokers submit your application to 20+ lenders—finding best rates, terms, and approval odds across conventional, FHA, VA, jumbo, and portfolio options.

Wholesale pricing runs 0.125%–0.375% below retail on identical loans. Wholesale lenders (United Wholesale Mortgage, Caliber, Flagstar) compete for broker volume by offering tighter margins than retail branches. Even after broker compensation (1%–2%), total APR often beats retail bank pricing.

Multi-lender shopping solves approval challenges. If Lender A declines or prices high, brokers move to Lender B or C without restarting your process. This matters for self-employed borrowers, investors, cash-out refinances over 80% LTV, or credit scores 620–680 where automated retail underwriting often declines.

Understanding NH Mortgage Brokers

How brokers work, fee structures, and wholesale pricing advantages.

Broker vs Bank Pricing Model

Retail banks lend their own money and price to cover overhead plus profit margin. Mortgage brokers access wholesale lenders who price 0.125%–0.375% below retail to compete for broker volume.

How Broker Compensation Works

NH brokers earn origination fees (1%–2% of loan amount) or yield spread premium from wholesale lenders. All compensation is disclosed in your Loan Estimate Section A.

Wholesale Lender Network Access

Brokers submit applications to United Wholesale Mortgage, Caliber, Flagstar, PennyMac, Angel Oak, and 15+ specialized lenders. Each lender competes on rate, fees, and underwriting overlays.

Portfolio Loan Advantages

NH brokers access portfolio lenders for scenarios retail banks auto-decline: self-employed with 1099 income, investors buying 5th+ property, cash-out refinances over 80% LTV, or credit scores 620–680.
