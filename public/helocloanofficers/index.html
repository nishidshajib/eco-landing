Frequently Asked HELOC Questions

Clear answers to common HELOC questions.

A Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC) is a revolving credit line secured by your homes equity. Unlike a fixed home equity loan, a HELOC lets you borrow, repay, and borrow again during the draw period (typically 10 years), similar to a credit card. You only pay interest on what you actually use, making it ideal for ongoing expenses like renovations, debt consolidation, or emergency reserves.

A HELOC is a second lien that supplements your existing first mortgage, preserving your current rate. A cash-out refinance replaces your entire mortgage with a new, larger loan. Choose a HELOC if you want to keep your low first mortgage rate; choose cash-out refinancing if current rates are lower than your existing mortgage or you want to consolidate all debt into one payment.

Most HELOC lenders require a minimum credit score of 620-680, though higher scores (700+) typically unlock better rates and terms. Understanding your middle credit score is crucial, as lenders use your mid-score among all three bureaus to determine HELOC eligibility, rate pricing, and maximum borrowing limits.
