Frequently Asked Cash-Out Refinancing Questions

Clear answers to common cash-out refinance questions.

Cash-out refinancing replaces your existing mortgage with a larger loan, allowing you to receive the difference in cash. Unlike rate-and-term refinancing (which only changes your rate or term), cash-out refinancing increases your loan balance to access home equity. Most lenders allow up to 80% LTV for conventional loans, though VA loans may permit up to 100% for eligible veterans.

Most conventional cash-out refinance lenders require a minimum credit score of 620, though scores of 680+ typically unlock the best rates and terms. Understanding your middle credit score is crucial, as lenders use your mid-score among all three bureaus to determine cash-out refinance eligibility and pricing.

Most lenders require at least 20% equity remaining after cash-out refinancing, meaning you can access up to 80% of your home value. For example, if your home is worth $400,000, you could potentially refinance up to $320,000. VA loans offer more flexibility, sometimes allowing up to 100% LTV for eligible veterans.
