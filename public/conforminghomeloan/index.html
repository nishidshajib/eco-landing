Conforming Loan FAQs

Straight answers to the questions lenders rarely explain clearly.

Conforming loans follow Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac guidelines, stay within FHFA loan limits, and can be sold into agency-backed securities. They offer competitive rates and flexible terms when you meet standard credit, income, and documentation requirements.

Most conforming loans allow debt-to-income ratios up to 45%, though AUS approvals sometimes stretch to 50% with strong credit, reserves, or large down payments. Understanding compensating factors helps you plan before applying.

No. First-time buyers can put as little as 3% down, and repeat buyers can use 5%. However, less than 20% down requires private mortgage insurance (PMI) until you reach 78-80% loan-to-value, at which point you can request removal or it drops automatically.

Yes, you can complete a conforming cash-out refinance up to 80% LTV on primary residences (lower for second homes and investment properties). Evaluate total costs, break-even points, and alternative equity products before locking.
