Texas Loan Officer Questions

Straightforward answers about how loan officers work and how they guide homebuyers.

Loan officers are typically employed by lenders and do not charge separate fees. The lender pays the loan officer salary/commission, funded through loan origination fees, interest rates, or processing fees. Never pay a loan officer directly—compensation flows from the lender.

Look for loan officers who explain clearly, don't pressure you, and take time to understand your financial goals. Verify licensing through NMLS and the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation. Read reviews and ask references about their experience.

Bank loan officers have access only to their bank's products and pricing. Independent loan officers access multiple lenders. Both can be good, but independent officers typically offer wider options. Compare offers from each to see who gives best pricing.
