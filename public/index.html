About California Home Rates

California mortgage rates vary significantly by loan type, region, and lender competition—a 0.25%–0.50% spread between highest and lowest quotes is normal for the same borrower on the same day. With California’s median home price exceeding $800,000 statewide (and $1.5M+ in Bay Area coastal markets), even a 0.125% rate difference translates to $70–150/month or $4,200–9,000 over five years.

Unlike national rate averages, California borrowers navigate high-balance conforming loan limits ($1,149,825 in most counties), jumbo loan pricing premiums (0.50%–0.75% above conforming), and regional lender competition that produces tighter pricing in Bay Area and Los Angeles compared to Sacramento or Inland Empire. The difference between shopping one bank’s retail rate and comparing 20–50 wholesale lenders through a mortgage broker is often 0.25%–0.375%—$175–260/month on a typical $900,000 California loan.

This directory provides California-specific rate intelligence: how high-balance conforming pricing works, which regions have the most competitive lender markets, how to compare APR (not just rate) to identify hidden lender fees, when to lock versus float in volatile California real estate cycles, and whether buying rate down with points or taking lender credits makes sense for your timeline. Rate shopping is the single highest-ROI activity California homebuyers can perform—30 minutes comparing Loan Estimates saves $15,000–25,000 over the life of the loan.
