Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common HELOC questions.

What's the difference between a HELOC and a home-equity loan?

A HELOC is a revolving line of credit with variable rates and flexible draws during a designated period. A home-equity loan provides a lump sum at a fixed rate. Your choice depends on whether you need ongoing access or a one-time disbursement.

What credit score do I need to qualify?

Most lenders prefer a middle credit score of at least 620, though stronger scores (680+) typically unlock better rates and terms. Visit MiddleCreditScore.com for strategies to optimize your credit profile before applying.

Will a HELOC affect my primary mortgage?

A HELOC is recorded as a second lien and doesn't replace your first mortgage. It sits subordinate in the lien hierarchy, meaning if you refinance your primary loan, you may need to re-subordinate the HELOC with lender approval.
