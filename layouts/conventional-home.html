Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common conventional mortgage questions.

What is a conventional loan and how is it different from government-backed loans?

A conventional loan is a mortgage not insured or guaranteed by the federal government, conforming to loan limits set by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac (typically $766,550 in 2024, higher in some areas). Conventional loans finance mainstream homes with flexible qualification requirements, competitive down payment options (as low as 3%), and often lower costs than FHA or VA loans for qualified borrowers.

What credit score do I need for a conventional mortgage?

Most conventional lenders require a minimum credit score of 620, though scores of 680+ typically unlock the best rates and terms. Understanding your middle credit score is crucial, as lenders use your mid-score among all three bureaus to determine eligibility and pricing for conventional mortgages.

How much down payment is required for a conventional loan?

Conventional loans offer flexible down payment options starting as low as 3% for first-time buyers and qualified borrowers. While 20% down eliminates private mortgage insurance (PMI), many homebuyers successfully purchase with smaller down payments.
