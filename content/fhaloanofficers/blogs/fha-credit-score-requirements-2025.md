## Official FHA Credit Score Minimums

The Federal Housing Administration sets baseline credit requirements, but they're more flexible than most people realize:

**FHA Minimum Credit Scores:**
- 580 or higher: Qualify for 3.5% down payment minimum
- 500-579: Qualify for 10% down payment minimum
- Below 500: Generally ineligible for FHA financing

These are the official rules, but here's where it gets complicated: FHA doesn't make loans directly. They insure loans made by FHA-approved lenders, and those lenders can impose stricter requirements called "overlays."

## Lender Overlays: The Real Credit Minimums

Most FHA-approved lenders require credit scores above the FHA minimums:

**Typical Lender Overlays:**
- Conservative lenders: 620-640 minimum
- Moderate lenders: 600-620 minimum
- Flexible lenders: 580-600 minimum
- Specialized lenders: Exact 580 minimum (rare)

I've seen borrowers with 582 credit scores get approved by one lender and denied by three others—same situation, different underwriting standards. This is why working with an FHA loan officer who knows which lenders accept lower scores is crucial.

## How Lenders Calculate Your FHA Credit Score

FHA uses your "middle score" from all three credit bureaus:

**Middle Score Calculation:**
1. Pull credit reports from Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion
2. Identify credit score from each bureau
3. Use the middle score (not average)

**Example:**
- Experian: 615
- Equifax: 605
- TransUnion: 625
- **Qualifying Score: 615** (the middle number)

If you're married or applying with a co-borrower, lenders use the lower middle score between both borrowers. So if you have a 640 middle score and your spouse has 590, your application is evaluated at 590.

This is why checking all three credit reports before applying is essential—you might discover one bureau has errors dragging down your middle score that you can dispute and fix.

## Credit Score Tiers and FHA Approval Odds

Not all credit scores in the 580+ range have equal approval chances:

**Credit Score 580-599: Challenging But Possible**
- Requires finding specialized lenders who go to FHA minimums
- May need larger down payment (10%) regardless of official 3.5% minimum
- Manual underwriting almost certain
- Strong compensating factors required (stable employment, low DTI, cash reserves)

**Credit Score 600-619: Moderate Approval Odds**
- Access to more lenders, but still limited pool
- 3.5% down payment achievable with most lenders
- Automated underwriting possible with strong compensating factors
- May require explanation letters for recent credit blemishes

**Credit Score 620-659: Good Approval Odds**
- Most FHA lenders will work with you
- Standard underwriting process applies
- Competitive interest rate pricing
- Can often overcome other credit issues with this score range

**Credit Score 660+: Excellent Approval Odds**
- All FHA lenders accessible
- Best interest rate pricing
- Should compare FHA vs. conventional to see which saves more
- May qualify for conventional with better long-term costs

## Compensating Factors That Help Lower Credit Scores

FHA underwriting evaluates your complete financial picture, not just credit scores. Strong compensating factors can offset lower scores:

**Powerful Compensating Factors:**
- Low debt-to-income ratio (under 35%)
- Significant cash reserves (6+ months of payments)
- Minimal increase in housing payment over current rent
- Stable employment history (2+ years same employer)
- Large down payment (10-20% instead of minimum 3.5%)
- No recent derogatory credit (2+ years since collections, late payments)

I've seen 585 credit score borrowers get approved because they had 15% down payment, 30% DTI, and 10 months of reserves. Meanwhile, 625 credit score borrowers with 3.5% down, 48% DTI, and two months reserves got denied.

## Manual Underwriting vs. Automated Approval

FHA loans go through either automated underwriting systems or manual underwriting:

**Automated Underwriting (AUS):**
- Computer algorithm evaluates application
- Faster approval (often same-day)
- More forgiving of lower credit scores with strong compensating factors
- Can approve situations manual underwriters might decline

**Manual Underwriting:**
- Human underwriter reviews entire file
- Required for credit scores below 580 or certain credit issues
- Stricter compensating factor requirements
- Longer approval timelines but more flexibility for non-traditional situations

**Manual Underwriting Requirements (Typical):**
- DTI ratio maximum 31% front-end / 43% back-end
- Minimum two months of cash reserves
- No more than 1x30 late payment in past 12 months
- Stable employment (2 years in same field)

## Credit Issues That Require Waiting Periods

Certain credit events impose mandatory waiting periods before FHA approval:

**FHA Waiting Periods:**
- **Chapter 7 Bankruptcy:** 2 years from discharge date
- **Chapter 13 Bankruptcy:** 1 year with payment plan (trustee approval required)
- **Foreclosure:** 3 years from completion date
- **Deed-in-Lieu of Foreclosure:** 3 years
- **Short Sale:** 3 years (may reduce to 1 year with extenuating circumstances)

**Extenuating Circumstances:**
FHA allows shorter waiting periods if you can document circumstances beyond your control:
- Job loss (not voluntary quit)
- Serious illness or medical bills
- Death of primary wage earner
- Divorce (must show financial impact)

Documentation requirements are strict—you need evidence the event caused your financial hardship and proof you've recovered financially since.

## Collections and Charge-Offs on FHA Loans

FHA doesn't require you to pay off collections and charge-offs before closing in most cases:

**FHA Collection Rules:**
- Medical collections: Generally ignored regardless of amount
- Non-medical collections: No payoff required if under $2,000 total
- Over $2,000 in collections: Lender may require payment plan or payoff
- Charge-offs: Included in DTI if showing minimum payment

**Strategic Approach:**
Don't pay off old collections right before applying—it can actually lower your credit score temporarily. Work with your FHA loan officer to determine if paying collections helps or hurts your specific situation.

## Student Loans and FHA Credit Scoring

Student loans in deferment or forbearance still affect your FHA loan:

**FHA Student Loan Rules:**
- Showing a monthly payment: Use that amount in DTI
- $0 payment (income-driven repayment): Use actual $0 payment
- No payment shown but not deferred: Use 0.5% of balance as payment
- Deferred loans: Use 0.5% of balance as payment

This can dramatically affect approval odds if you have large student loan balances. A $50,000 student loan in deferment counts as $250/month payment in DTI calculations.

## Improving Your Credit Score Before Applying

If your credit score is close to key thresholds (580, 600, 620), strategic improvements can open better options:

**Fast Credit Improvements (30-60 Days):**
- Pay down credit card balances below 30% utilization
- Dispute any errors on credit reports
- Become authorized user on family member's excellent credit card
- Pay off small collections under $500

**Medium-Term Improvements (3-6 Months):**
- Pay all bills on time (most important factor)
- Diversify credit mix (add installment loan if only have credit cards)
- Avoid new credit inquiries
- Keep old accounts open (length of history matters)

**Long-Term Improvements (6-12+ Months):**
- Let derogatory marks age (older = less impact)
- Build positive payment history consistently
- Reduce overall debt load
- Consider credit counseling if overwhelmed

## Non-Traditional Credit for FHA Loans

If you have limited or no credit history, FHA accepts non-traditional credit documentation:

**Non-Traditional Credit Sources:**
- Rent payment history (12 months of on-time payments)
- Utility bills (12 months)
- Cell phone bills (12 months)
- Auto insurance payments (12 months)
- Childcare or tuition payments

You need at least three non-traditional trade lines with 12-month history each. This path typically requires manual underwriting and stricter compensating factors.

## Credit Scores and FHA Mortgage Insurance Costs

Here's something most borrowers don't realize: FHA mortgage insurance premiums are the same regardless of credit score:

**FHA Mortgage Insurance Premiums:**
- Upfront premium: 1.75% of loan amount (can be financed)
- Annual premium: 0.55%-0.85% depending on loan amount and LTV (not credit score)

This is a huge advantage for lower credit score borrowers compared to conventional loans, where PMI premiums increase dramatically with lower scores.

## When to Choose FHA vs. Conventional with Middle Credit

If your credit score is 620-680, you might qualify for both FHA and conventional:

**FHA Advantages:**
- Lower credit score acceptance (580 vs. 620)
- Lower down payment (3.5% vs. 3-5%)
- Same mortgage insurance cost regardless of credit score
- More flexible debt-to-income ratios

**Conventional Advantages:**
- PMI can be removed at 20% equity
- Better overall costs if you have 660+ credit and 10%+ down
- Higher loan limits in expensive areas
- No upfront mortgage insurance premium

An FHA loan officer can run both scenarios to show you which loan type saves more money over your actual expected holding period.

## Credit Repair Companies: Worth It?

Before paying a credit repair company, understand what they can and can't do:

**Credit Repair Companies Can:**
- Dispute errors on your credit reports
- Write dispute letters on your behalf
- Track progress of disputes

**Credit Repair Companies Cannot:**
- Remove accurate negative information
- Guarantee any specific score improvement
- Do anything you can't do yourself for free

**DIY Alternative:**
1. Pull free credit reports from AnnualCreditReport.com
2. Identify errors or questionable items
3. Dispute directly with credit bureaus (free)
4. Follow up monthly until resolved

If you're overwhelmed, a reputable credit counseling agency (look for nonprofit status) offers free guidance without the high fees of credit repair companies.
