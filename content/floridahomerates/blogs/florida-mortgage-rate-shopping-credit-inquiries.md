You want to shop multiple Florida lenders to find the best rate. But you've heard that every credit check hurts your score. Can you compare lenders without tanking your credit? Yes—if you do it right. Here's how.

## How Credit Inquiries Affect Your Score

When a lender pulls your credit, it creates a "hard inquiry" on your report. Too many hard inquiries signal you're desperate for credit, which lowers your score.

**Typical Impact:**
- 1 hard inquiry: 5–10 point drop
- Multiple inquiries spread over months: 20–50 point drop
- Multiple inquiries for same purpose in short window: 5–10 point drop total (counted as one)

The key is the "rate shopping window"—a period where multiple mortgage inquiries count as a single inquiry.

## The Rate Shopping Window (14–45 Days)

Credit scoring models recognize that smart borrowers shop multiple lenders. To encourage this, they group mortgage inquiries within a specific timeframe as one inquiry.

**FICO Score 8 and Older Models:**
14-day window (some models use 45 days)

**VantageScore:**
14-day window

**What This Means:**
Apply to 3, 5, or 10 lenders within 14–45 days (depending on scoring model). Your credit score treats them as one inquiry, not ten.

**How to Use This:**
- Week 1: Check credit, gather documents, get pre-qualified (soft pull, no impact)
- Week 2–3: Submit full applications to 3–5 lenders (within 14 days of first hard pull)
- Week 4: Compare Loan Estimates, choose lender

You get multiple rate quotes with minimal credit score impact.

## Hard Pull vs. Soft Pull: Know the Difference

**Soft Pull (No Credit Impact):**
- Pre-qualification estimates
- You checking your own credit
- Lender checking rates without full application
- Background checks for employment

**Hard Pull (5–10 Point Impact):**
- Full mortgage application
- Car loan application
- Credit card application
- Personal loan application

When rate shopping, ask lenders: "Is this a soft pull or hard pull?" If they soft pull first, you can see estimated rates without credit impact. Then you choose 3 lenders for full applications (hard pulls) within the 14-day window.

## Step-by-Step: How to Shop Florida Mortgage Rates

**Step 1: Pre-Qualify with Soft Pulls (No Credit Impact)**
Contact 5–7 Florida lenders. Ask:
- "Can you pre-qualify me with a soft credit pull?"
- "What rate and fees would I likely qualify for?"

Get rough estimates without credit hits. Narrow to 3 lenders.

**Step 2: Gather Your Documents**
Before formal applications, have ready:
- 30 days pay stubs
- 2 years W-2s
- 2 years tax returns (if self-employed)
- 2 months bank/asset statements
- ID and Social Security card

Complete documentation speeds up the process and ensures accurate rate quotes.

**Step 3: Submit Full Applications Within 14 Days**
Apply to your top 3 lenders within a 14-day window. All hard pulls count as one inquiry.

**Step 4: Request Written Loan Estimates**
Lenders must provide Loan Estimates within 3 business days of application. Get all three in writing.

**Step 5: Compare APR, Fees, and Terms**
Don't just compare rates. Compare:
- APR (true cost including fees)
- Total lender fees (Section A of Loan Estimate)
- Lock period and expiration date
- Closing cost estimates

**Step 6: Lock with Your Chosen Lender**
Once you've compared, lock your rate with the best lender. Others can wait—you don't owe them anything.

## What to Ask Each Florida Lender

Don't just ask "What's your rate?" Get specific:

**1. "What rate and APR do I qualify for based on my actual credit score, LTV, and DTI?"**
Forces honest pricing, not advertised teaser rates.

**2. "What are your total origination charges?"**
Section A of Loan Estimate. This is where lenders compete.

**3. "How long is the rate lock, and what do extensions cost?"**
30-day, 45-day, 60-day locks? Free extensions if lender causes delays?

**4. "Do you have float-down options if rates drop after I lock?"**
Know the terms upfront.

**5. "What's your average closing timeline for this loan type?"**
Faster closings mean less risk of rate lock expiration.

**6. "Are there any fees I can negotiate or remove?"**
Processing fees, document prep fees, junk fees—push back.

## How Many Lenders Should You Shop?

**Minimum: 3 Lenders**
You need at least 3 to compare pricing. One lender might lowball to get your business, another might be overpriced, one is likely fair market value.

**Sweet Spot: 3–5 Lenders**
More than 5 gets overwhelming. You're drowning in paperwork and you'll see diminishing returns—rates cluster around market price.

**Maximum: 7 Lenders**
Beyond 7, you're wasting time. The difference between the 5th and 7th best rate quote is negligible.

Target 3–5 lenders within your rate shopping window.

## Types of Florida Lenders to Compare

Don't shop just one lender type. Compare across categories:

**Big Banks (Wells Fargo, Bank of America, Chase):**
- Pros: Name recognition, lots of branches, online tools
- Cons: Higher fees, slower processing, rigid guidelines

**Credit Unions:**
- Pros: Lower fees, member-focused, competitive rates
- Cons: Membership requirements, fewer loan programs

**Online Lenders (Rocket Mortgage, Better.com):**
- Pros: Fast, tech-enabled, competitive rates
- Cons: No local service, harder to get questions answered

**Mortgage Brokers:**
- Pros: Shop multiple lenders for you, access to wholesale rates
- Cons: Broker fees, potential conflicts of interest

**Local/Regional Banks:**
- Pros: Community-focused, flexible underwriting, relationship banking
- Cons: Higher rates than national lenders sometimes

Compare at least 3 lender types to see the full pricing spectrum.

## Red Flags When Rate Shopping

Walk away if:

**Lender Refuses to Provide Written Rate Quote**
Everything should be in writing. Verbal promises mean nothing.

**Lender Won't Disclose Fees Upfront**
If they dodge fee questions, they're hiding something.

**Rate Quote Changes Every Time You Call**
Markets move, but if your quote changes drastically day-to-day with no market movement, they're playing games.

**Lender Pressures Immediate Application**
"Apply now or this rate disappears!" is a scare tactic. Professional lenders give you time to decide.

**Loan Estimate Doesn't Match Quote**
You were quoted 6.50%, Loan Estimate shows 6.75%. Demand explanation or walk.

**Lender Requests Upfront Fees Before Application**
Legitimate lenders don't charge application fees before issuing a Loan Estimate (some charge at application, most charge at closing).

## How to Organize Your Rate Shopping

You're comparing 3–5 lenders. Don't let the paperwork overwhelm you.

**Create a Comparison Spreadsheet:**

| Lender | Rate | APR | Origination Fee | Other Fees | Total Closing Costs | Lock Period | Notes |
|--------|------|-----|----------------|-----------|-------------------|------------|-------|
| Lender A | 6.50% | 6.71% | $2,500 | $1,200 | $8,700 | 45 days | Fast closing |
| Lender B | 6.625% | 6.69% | $1,500 | $900 | $7,400 | 45 days | Best APR |
| Lender C | 6.50% | 6.82% | $4,000 | $1,800 | $10,800 | 30 days | Too expensive |

Compare APR first (shows true cost). Then check origination fees (negotiable). Then total closing costs.

Winner: Lender B—slightly higher rate but lowest total cost.

## Common Rate Shopping Mistakes

**Mistake #1: Shopping Rate Alone**
Rate doesn't tell the full story. Compare APR and fees.

**Mistake #2: Shopping Outside the Credit Window**
Applying to 3 lenders over 3 months = 3 hard inquiries = score damage. Do it within 14 days.

**Mistake #3: Not Getting Everything in Writing**
Verbal rate quotes change at closing. Demand written Loan Estimates.

**Mistake #4: Falling for Teaser Rates**
"3.99% available!" usually requires buying 3 points, 760+ credit, 30% down, and doesn't include fees. Not applicable to your scenario.

**Mistake #5: Skipping Local Lenders**
Everyone checks the big online lenders. Local Florida banks and credit unions sometimes have better pricing and service.

## How to Negotiate After Shopping

You've got 3 Loan Estimates. Lender B is best, but Lender A is close. Can you negotiate?

**Yes. Here's How:**

**1. Show Lender A the Better Offer**
"Lender B quoted 6.69% APR with $7,400 closing costs. Can you match or beat it?"

**2. Ask to Reduce Origination Fees**
"Your rate is good, but your origination fee is $1,000 higher. Can you drop it to match Lender B?"

**3. Request Lender Credits**
"If you can't lower fees, can you give me a lender credit to cover appraisal or title costs?"

**4. Leverage Your Strengths**
"I'm a strong borrower—760 credit, 25% down, stable employment. What's your best pricing?"

Some lenders negotiate. Some don't. Worth asking.

## How Long Does the Credit Inquiry Stay on Your Report?

Hard inquiries remain on your credit report for 2 years, but they only affect your score for 12 months.

**Months 1–12:** Inquiry impacts score (5–10 point drop)
**Months 13–24:** Inquiry visible but no score impact
**After 24 months:** Inquiry falls off report entirely

If you shopped rates in January 2024, the inquiries stop affecting your score in January 2025 and disappear entirely in January 2026.
