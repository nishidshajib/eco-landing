## What Are High-Balance Conventional Loans?

A **high-balance conventional loan** is a mortgage that exceeds the national baseline conforming limit but stays within the **maximum cap set by the Federal Housing Finance Agency (FHFA)** for designated high-cost areas. These loans are still eligible for purchase by **Fannie Mae** and **Freddie Mac**, but they carry slightly higher pricing adjustments due to increased loan size.

In 2025, the national **baseline conforming limit** for a one-unit property is projected to be **$795,000**, with high-cost counties reaching up to **$1,192,500** depending on local median home prices. High-balance conventional loans serve borrowers in these pricier markets who want to avoid jumbo underwriting complexity while retaining the benefits of conventional financing.

---

## Why the FHFA Adjusts Limits Annually

The FHFA recalculates conforming and high-balance loan limits each year based on its **House Price Index (HPI)**, which tracks home appreciation nationwide. As housing costs rise, loan limits expand to maintain accessibility and affordability.

### Factors influencing 2025 loan limits:
- **Rising home prices** in major metro regions (California, New York, Florida, Texas)  
- **Higher construction costs** driving up median values  
- **Federal policy** supporting middle-income housing affordability  

These adjustments ensure that creditworthy borrowers can finance homes without resorting to jumbo loans, which typically have stricter documentation and higher reserve requirements.

## Qualification Requirements for 2025 High-Balance Loans

Borrowers must meet conforming credit and income guidelines, but slightly enhanced standards apply due to the larger loan amounts.

### Minimum Requirements
- **Credit Score:** Minimum **680–700** depending on LTV and occupancy  
- **Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI):** Typically **≤ 45%** (may extend to 50% with compensating factors)  
- **Down Payment:** As low as **5% for primary residences**, 10–15% for second homes  
- **Reserves:** 2–6 months of principal, interest, taxes, and insurance (PITIA)  
- **Property Type:** One-unit primary residences and second homes eligible  

### Borrower Tip
Your **Middle Credit Score®**—the median of your three FICO® scores—directly impacts rate pricing and approval strength. Improving it before application can lower total cost of borrowing.

## Credit, Income, and Appraisal Considerations

Because high-balance loans are risk-adjusted, borrowers may see **Loan-Level Price Adjustments (LLPAs)** that depend on credit, LTV, and occupancy type. To minimize these costs:  
- Maintain **Middle Credit Score® ≥ 740**  
- Keep **LTV ≤ 80%** where possible  
- Document consistent, verifiable income  
- Choose a **qualified property appraiser** familiar with your county’s high-cost thresholds  

Appraisal values are particularly important since FHFA loan eligibility depends on **conforming pricing categories**, not jumbo risk layers.
