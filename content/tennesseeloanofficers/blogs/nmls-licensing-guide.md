## Tennessee Loan Officer NMLS Licensing: Complete Guide

Tennessee mortgage loan officers must be licensed through NMLS (Nationwide Multistate Licensing System) and comply with state and federal regulations. **Understanding licensing requirements, testing, and continuing education helps borrowers verify loan officer credentials and ensures working with qualified, compliant MLOs.**

### What is NMLS Licensing?

**NMLS (Nationwide Multistate Licensing System):**

Federal registry and licensing system for mortgage loan originators created by the SAFE Act (Secure and Fair Enforcement for Mortgage Licensing Act) of 2008.

**Purpose:**
- Standardize mortgage loan officer licensing nationwide
- Protect consumers from unqualified or fraudulent MLOs
- Track loan officer employment, education, and disciplinary history
- Enable multi-state licensing for mortgage professionals

**Who requires NMLS license:**
- ✅ All mortgage loan originators (MLOs)
- ✅ Mortgage brokers and companies
- ✅ Bank/credit union mortgage loan officers
- ✅ Anyone taking mortgage applications or negotiating terms

**Who is exempt:**
- Administrative staff (processors, underwriters)
- Real estate agents (unless also originating loans)
- Attorneys performing closings only

### Tennessee NMLS Licensing Requirements

**Step 1: Pre-Licensing Education (20 Hours)**

**NMLS-approved courses covering:**
- Federal mortgage laws and regulations (3 hours)
- Ethics (3 hours)
- Non-traditional mortgage products (2 hours)
- Electives (12 hours): Underwriting, programs, fraud prevention

**Providers:** Multiple NMLS-approved education providers (online and in-person)
- Mortgage Educators & Compliance
- 360training
- Affinity Education
- OnCourse Learning

**Cost:** $200-$400 typically

**Timeline:** Self-paced online (can complete in 1-2 weeks); in-person varies by provider

---

**Step 2: National NMLS Exam**

**SAFE MLO Test (National Component):**
- Questions: 125 (115 scored + 10 experimental)
- Passing score: 75% (86 correct answers minimum)
- Time limit: 3 hours
- Format: Multiple choice
- Cost: $110 per attempt

**Content areas:**
- Federal mortgage-related laws (23%)
- General mortgage knowledge (23%)
- Mortgage loan origination activities (25%)
- Ethics (14%)
- Uniform State Content (15%)

**Pass rate:** ~60% first attempt; ~75% eventual pass rate

**Retake policy:**
- Failed by <5 points: Wait 30 days before retake
- Failed by 5+ points: No waiting period (can retake immediately)
- Unlimited attempts (must retake within 6 months of initial attempt)

---

**Step 3: Tennessee State Exam**

**Tennessee-Specific Component:**
- Questions: 25
- Passing score: 75% (19 correct answers minimum)
- Time limit: 1 hour
- Format: Multiple choice
- Cost: Included in $110 national exam fee

**Content areas:**
- Tennessee mortgage lending laws
- Tennessee Department of Financial Institutions regulations
- Tennessee-specific consumer protection laws
- Tennessee foreclosure and deed of trust procedures

**Testing centers:** Prometric testing centers across Tennessee (Nashville, Memphis, Knoxville, Chattanooga, and other cities)

---

**Step 4: Background & Credit Checks**

**FBI Criminal Background Check:**
- Fingerprints required (submitted through NMLS)
- Cost: $36.25 (FBI processing)
- Checks federal and state criminal history
- Disqualifying offenses: Felony in past 7 years, fraud/dishonesty crimes

**Credit Report Review:**
- NMLS pulls credit report during application
- Cost: Included in NMLS processing fees
- Reviews: Bankruptcies, foreclosures, judgments, payment history
- Not automatic disqualification but must be explained

**Disqualifying factors:**
- Felony conviction involving fraud, dishonesty, breach of trust (past 7 years)
- Revoked mortgage license in another state
- Outstanding tax liens or judgments
- Foreclosure within past 3 years (case-by-case)

---

**Step 5: Surety Bond**

**Tennessee requirement:**
- Bond amount: $25,000 minimum (can be higher based on loan volume)
- Purpose: Consumer protection (funds available if MLO commits fraud)
- Cost: $100-$300 annually (depends on credit, experience)
- Provider: Surety bond companies (many available online)

---

**Step 6: Submit NMLS License Application**

**Application through NMLS Consumer Access:**
- Create NMLS account
- Complete MU4 form (mortgage industry Uniform Application)
- Upload pre-licensing education certificates
- Submit exam results (automatic if passed through NMLS)
- Pay Tennessee license fee: $200 (initial); $100 (renewal)
- Provide employment verification (sponsoring company)

**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks from application to license approval (if all requirements met)

---

### Tennessee Loan Officer License Maintenance

**Annual Renewal:**

Tennessee MLO licenses expire December 31 each year.

**Renewal requirements:**
- Complete 8 hours continuing education (by November 30)
- Pay renewal fee: $100
- Update employment information if changed
- Maintain surety bond
- Pass background check review (if required)

**Renewal processing:** Automatic if all requirements met; renew through NMLS by December 31

---

**Continuing Education (CE) Requirements:**

**8 Hours Annual CE:**
- Federal mortgage laws and regulations: 3 hours
- Ethics: 2 hours
- Non-traditional mortgage products: 2 hours
- Electives: 1 hour

**Approved providers:** Same as pre-licensing education
- Online or in-person
- Cost: $50-$150 per course
- Deadline: Complete by November 30 (for December 31 renewal)

**Failure to complete CE:** License expires; must reapply (including retaking exams)

### Tennessee Loan Officer Disciplinary Actions

**Tennessee Department of Financial Institutions oversight:**

**Common violations leading to discipline:**
- Fraud or misrepresentation to borrowers
- Failure to disclose compensation or fees
- Steering borrowers to unsuitable products
- Falsifying loan applications
- Failure to maintain license (CE, renewals)
- Criminal convictions
- Operating without valid license

**Disciplinary actions:**
- Fines: $1,000-$25,000 per violation
- License suspension: Temporary (30-90 days typical)
- License revocation: Permanent ban from mortgage lending
- Consent orders: Agreements to correct violations

**Public record:** All disciplinary actions visible on NMLS Consumer Access

### How Borrowers Can Verify Tennessee Loan Officer Licensing

**Step-by-step verification:**

1. **Request NMLS number** from loan officer
2. **Visit NMLSConsumerAccess.org**
3. **Search** by NMLS number or name
4. **Review profile:**
   - License status (active in Tennessee?)
   - Issue date (experience indicator)
   - Employment history (current employer listed?)
   - Disciplinary actions (any violations?)
   - Other states licensed (multi-state MLO?)

**What to look for:**

✅ **Green checkmarks:**
- "Tennessee - Licensed Mortgage Loan Originator"
- Active status
- Current employer listed

❌ **Red flags:**
- License expired or inactive
- Disciplinary actions or consent orders
- Employment status "Inactive" (not currently sponsored)
- Recent license (if claiming 10+ years experience)

### Common Tennessee Loan Officer Licensing Questions

**Q: Can I originate loans in Tennessee without NMLS license?**

**A:** No. Federal and Tennessee law require NMLS licensing for all mortgage loan originators. Operating without license is illegal; penalties include fines, criminal charges, and permanent ban.

---

**Q: How long does Tennessee NMLS license approval take?**

**A:** 4-8 weeks after submitting complete application (education, exam, background check, surety bond). Background check is longest component; can take 2-6 weeks.

---

**Q: What happens if I fail the NMLS exam?**

**A:** Retake allowed. If failed by <5 points, wait 30 days before retake. If failed by 5+ points, can retake immediately. Must retake within 6 months of initial attempt or restart education.

---

**Q: Can Tennessee loan officers work in multiple states?**

**A:** Yes. Apply for licenses in additional states through same NMLS account. Must pass each state's exam and pay respective fees. Manage all licenses through single NMLS account.

---

**Q: What if I don't complete continuing education by deadline?**

**A:** License expires December 31 if CE not completed by November 30. Must reapply for license (including retaking national + state exams). No grace period.

---

**Q: How do I check if a Tennessee loan officer is licensed?**

**A:** Visit NMLSConsumerAccess.org. Search by loan officer name or NMLS number. Verify license active in Tennessee, check employment, review disciplinary history.

---

**Q: What is the difference between MLO and mortgage broker license?**

**A:** MLO license = individual loan originator (required for all loan officers). Mortgage broker license = company license (required for brokerages). Both required for brokerage operations.
